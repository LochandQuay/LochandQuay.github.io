Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsps coconut aminos or reduced-sodium tamari
  • 2 Tbsps coconut oil (liquid)
  • 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 Tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 package (8 ounces) tempeh, cut into cubes
  • 1 medium butternut squash (about 4 cups), peeled, deseeded and cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 medium sweet yellow onion, chopped
  • 3/4 cup chopped portobello mushroom
  • Sea salt and pepper, to taste
  • Crushed red pepper flakes, to taste
  • Sriracha or chili sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Whisk tamari/coconut aminos, coconut oil, apple cider vinegar, ginger and garlic in a small bowl.
  3. User hands to gently toss tempeh, butternut squash, onion and mushrooms in a square baking dish or a baking sheet.
  4. Pour tamari mixture over tempeh, squash, onion and mushrooms and toss to coat. Sprinkle on sea salt, ground pepper and crushed red pepper flakes to taste.
  5. Cover baking dish/sheet with aluminum foil and bake for 35 minutes.
  6. Pull baking dish out, remove and discard aluminum foil, stir and place back in the oven for another 20 minutes or until butternut squash is cooked to your liking.
  7. Remove from baking sheet and serve over quinoa, rice or greens.
